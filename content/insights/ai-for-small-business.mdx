There's a gap between what the internet tells you AI can do and what it actually does for a business your size. Let's close that gap.

## What AI Is Not

AI is not a magic button that replaces your employees. It's not going to run your business while you sit on a beach. And it's definitely not the thing you saw on LinkedIn where someone claims they "10x'd their revenue with ChatGPT."

Most of those stories are either exaggerated, don't apply to your type of business, or describe a shortcut that breaks the first time something goes wrong.

## What AI Actually Does Well

Here's where AI makes a real difference for businesses with 5-100 employees:

**Research and reporting.** Instead of spending 2 hours pulling numbers from three different systems and putting them into a spreadsheet, AI can do it in minutes. Not because it's smarter than you — because it doesn't get tired and it doesn't make copy-paste errors.

**Document generation.** SOPs, employee handbooks, proposals, customer communications — anything with a predictable structure can be drafted by AI and reviewed by a human. You go from a blank page to a 90% draft in seconds instead of hours.

**Data organization.** If your business data lives in spreadsheets, email threads, and sticky notes, AI can help you build a single source of truth. Not a giant software implementation — just a dashboard that shows you what matters.

**Customer communication.** Not chatbots. Actual helpful tools — automated follow-ups, appointment reminders, personalized outreach based on what a customer actually bought or asked about.

## Where to Start

The biggest mistake small businesses make with AI is trying to do everything at once. Here's a better approach:

1. Pick the one task that eats the most time in your week
2. Ask yourself: "Is this task repetitive and structured?"
3. If yes, that's your AI starting point

For most businesses, that's either reporting, document creation, or customer follow-up. Start there. Get one thing working. Then expand.
