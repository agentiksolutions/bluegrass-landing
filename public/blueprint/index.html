Step 01

Discovery — Know Yourself First

Before touching any AI tool, answer these 5 questions honestly. They become the input that shapes everything Claude builds for you.

1

Time Audit

How do you spend most of your time?

Maps your daily reality. AI can only help if it knows where your hours actually go. Be honest — this is your starting point.

2

Automation Targets

What repetitive tasks do you want to eliminate?

These are automation gold mines. The stuff you do over and over that follows a pattern — AI eats these for breakfast.

3

Energy Drains

What do you NOT enjoy doing?

Energy drains = delegation targets. AI doesn't get bored, frustrated, or burnt out. Let it handle the stuff you dread.

4

The Backlog

What do you NOT have time to do?

The "someday" list. Projects, ideas, improvements you've been meaning to get to. AI compresses weeks of effort into hours.

5

Skill Gaps

What do you NOT have the expertise to do?

AI bridges skill gaps instantly. Coding, writing, design, data analysis, legal research — capabilities on demand.

What You Do With These Answers

YOUR FIRST MESSAGE TO CLAUDE

Paste your answers directly into Claude and say something like:

"Here's how I spend my time, what I want to automate, what I don't enjoy, what I don't have time for, and what I don't know how to do. Based on all of this, help me build a system that works for my life."

Claude will propose workflows, automations, and tools tailored exactly to you. This is how it starts.

Step 04

How to Communicate — Talking to Claude

You don't need special syntax. Talk to Claude like a smart colleague. But a little structure goes a long way.

The Prompt Formula

1
ContextTell Claude WHO you are and what you're working on.
2
TaskTell Claude exactly WHAT you want. Be specific.
3
FormatTell Claude HOW you want it. A doc? Table? Code?
4
ConstraintsTell Claude what to AVOID or include. Tone, length, style.
5
IterateReview → Refine → Repeat. "Change X, keep Y, add Z."
Example Prompt

"I run a landscaping business with 8 employees. I need a weekly schedule template that accounts for weather delays, shows job assignments per crew, and fits on one printed page. Keep it simple and clean."

Communication Tips

Talk naturally

Don't try to "code" your prompts. Plain English works.

Give examples

"Something like this..." helps Claude understand your taste.

Say what's wrong

"Too formal" beats "make it better" every time.

One thing at a time

Complex projects? Break them into steps.

Use follow-ups

Claude remembers the whole conversation. Build on it.

Copy/paste context

Drop in emails, docs, data — Claude reads all of it.

Use the mic GAME-CHANGER

Tap the microphone and TALK. 3x faster than typing.

Set expectations

"I'm a beginner" calibrates the response level.

Step 06

Tips, Tricks & Power Moves

Once you're comfortable, these will 10x your effectiveness.

Use Your Voice TOP TIP

Tap the microphone icon. TALK your prompts instead of typing. 3x faster and more natural. You can ramble — Claude understands messy speech.

Upload Everything

Drag PDFs, images, spreadsheets directly into the chat. "Here's my lease — summarize the key terms" — done in 10 seconds.

Use Projects

Create Projects for different areas — Work, Personal, Side Hustle. Each remembers its own context. Keeps everything organized.

Memory is Real

Claude remembers things about you across conversations. The more you use it, the less you explain each time.

Copy & Iterate

Don't aim for perfect on shot one. Get a draft, review, give feedback, iterate. Speed over perfection.

Web Search

Claude searches the web for current info. News, prices, research — real-time data in your conversation.

File Creation

Claude creates real files — Word docs, spreadsheets, PDFs, code. Ask for it and download the actual file.

Treat It Like a Partner

Have a CONVERSATION. Explain your situation. Ask for opinions. Push back. That's where the magic happens.

Your First Week

Getting Started — Day by Day

A concrete 7-day plan to go from zero to comfortable.

DAY 1

Answer the 5 discovery questions. Sign up at claude.ai. Send your first message with your answers. Ask Claude to suggest how it can help you.

DAY 2

Try the mic — speak a prompt instead of typing. Upload a document you've been meaning to deal with. Let Claude summarize or rewrite it.

DAY 3

Ask Claude to help with a real work task. An email, a report, a plan. See how fast the first draft comes back. Iterate on it.

DAY 4

Create your first Project. Give it context about one area of your life. Start building a go-to workspace.

DAY 5

Ask Claude to BUILD something — a tracker, a template, a tool. See Artifacts in action. Download the file and use it.

DAY 6

Explore web search. Ask Claude about today's news, your industry, a decision you're making. Real-time info in conversation.

DAY 7

Reflect: What saved you the most time? What surprised you? Double down on what worked. You're now an AI user.
